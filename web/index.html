    How this router decides what's safe

    Every street and path in Cambridge & Somerville gets a protection class by merging several sources, most-trusted first:

    1. Your own sketchy marks (right-click / long-press a street) — strongest;
    2. Cambridge's official Bike Facilities GIS — explicit facility types (separated lane, buffered, painted, path…), updated by the city as construction finishes;
    3. MassDOT's statewide Bike Inventory and Bike Level of Traffic Stress layers (the Furth/Mekuria LTS methodology);
    4. OpenStreetMap tags (cycleway=track/lane, road class, speed limits) — the base network, and often the freshest source since local mappers add new lanes quickly. Facilities known only from OSM draw dashed on the map.

    Each class costs a multiplier on its length — how much longer a detour the router will accept to avoid it. For the strictest profile (young kids):

    Two per-trip controls sit on top: 🛡 avoid lane types raises chosen types (e.g. painted lanes) to a near-prohibitive ×30 — "as much as possible", never a hard ban — and 🚶 ok to walk lets the router bridge safe segments with short bike-walking stretches (sidewalk pace, dismount friction included) when that beats a long ride-around; walking stretches draw as white dashes, appear in the cue sheet, and are announced in navigation.

    On top of that: segments near bicycle crashes (MassDOT IMPACT records, 2021–present, ~850 in these two cities) cost up to 2.5× extra; crossing a busy street without a signal adds a large penalty (a small one when signalized); Somerville's official high-crash corridors add 1.5×; and with "prefer flat" each meter of climb costs 12 m-equivalent (doubled over 4% grade, from ~14 m-resolution terrain data). Routes are graded A–F on average kid-stress per meter — the same scale regardless of profile, so grades are always comparable.

    Limitations

    Data can lag reality — construction, temporary closures, and brand-new lanes may be wrong for a few weeks (right-click any street to correct it for yourself). A painted lane's on-the-ground condition (parked cars, debris) isn't in any dataset. Crash records reflect where people already ride, not pure risk. This is a planning aid: always ride to conditions and use your own judgment, especially with kids.
